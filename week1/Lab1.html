3. Characteristics of Bad Requirements

The OpenUP documentation lists these "Requirements Pitfalls".

  • Ambiguous. Requirement is not clearly stated.
  • Multiple requirements written as one. "and", "or", "but"
  • Exceptions or "escape clause". Requirements containing wording such as "unless ...", "except when ...", "if", "but", "although".
  • Long sentences or references to other documents. Lead to errors.
  • Premature Design. Requirements that specify implementation or design details, such as input details, database fields.
  • Mixing different kinds of requirements in one.
  • Vague, undefined terms such as "easy to use", "user friendly", "approximately".
  • Cannot be verified or impossible to achieve. Such as "system should never crash". Even if the system is error-free, how can you verify this?

How to Discover Requirements?

Some techniques for discovering requirements are:

  • Interview users
  • Brainstorming and workshops involving stakeholders
  • Observation. Observe people using system (or performing task system will do)
  • Work in the target environment yourself!
  • Demonstrate prototype to stakeholders.
  • User complaints and suggestions.
  • Unintended uses of existing app. This may be a source of innovation.
  • Examine similar or competing products.
Source: See the OpenUP documentation on se.cpe.ku.ac.th. Under OpenUP Disciplines, Requirements, Requirements Gathering Techniques.
